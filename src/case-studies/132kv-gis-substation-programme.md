## The challenge

The client was progressing a series of network reinforcement projects to increase network capacity, support new customer connections and enable future growth.

One of the schemes involved the redevelopment of an existing 132kV substation. The project presented several interconnected challenges, including the integration of emerging switchgear technology, complex land requirements, stakeholder coordination and planning constraints. Bringing these competing workstreams together into a clear, deliverable strategy was essential to maintaining programme momentum and securing timely approval.

---

## Key considerations

### Complex technical requirements

The scheme incorporated emerging switchgear technology and required alignment with industry standards, operational requirements and delivery partner processes. Technical decisions needed to balance innovation, compliance and long-term operational performance.

### Land and stakeholder coordination

The project required a combination of permanent and temporary land arrangements involving multiple landowners and stakeholders, alongside planning, environmental and access considerations.

### Programme certainty

The client needed confidence that the preferred delivery route was technically robust, commercially viable and capable of being delivered without impacting the wider programme.

---

## Our approach

Lockrose provided senior programme management and strategic project leadership, coordinating the various technical, commercial and stakeholder workstreams required to develop a viable delivery strategy.

### Developing a preferred delivery strategy

Lockrose led the options assessment and design review process, bringing together technical, commercial and programme considerations to identify the preferred solution. This enabled approval to be secured within four weeks and established a clear route into delivery.

### Managing technical interfaces

Lockrose coordinated targeted technical assessments and stakeholder engagement activities to support technology integration, compliance requirements and delivery planning. This reduced uncertainty, clarified technical requirements and maintained progress across critical programme interfaces.

### Coordinating land and stakeholder requirements

Working closely with project stakeholders, Lockrose developed practical solutions to address land and access requirements, enabling early project activities to progress and reducing delivery risk. Stakeholder engagement activities were aligned with programme objectives to support efficient decision-making and timely approvals.
