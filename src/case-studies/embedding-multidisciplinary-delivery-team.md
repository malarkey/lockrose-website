## The challenge

The project combined complex tunnelling works, cable installation and multiple contractor interfaces within a live transmission network environment.

To maintain delivery momentum, the client required more than individual resources. They needed an integrated team capable of providing programme controls, construction assurance, quality oversight and coordinated support across the project.

Maintaining visibility of project performance, managing contractor interfaces and ensuring consistent quality standards were critical to successful delivery. The client required clear reporting, proactive risk management and independent assurance to support effective decision-making throughout construction.

---

## Key considerations

### Programme control

The scale and complexity of the project required robust planning and programme controls. Accurate forecasting, clear reporting and visibility of key dependencies were essential to maintaining confidence in delivery.

### Construction assurance

Complex tunnelling and infrastructure works required ongoing oversight to monitor progress, verify compliance and identify emerging issues before they could affect programme performance.

### Quality management
Maintaining consistent quality standards across multiple contractors and work packages required a structured approach to inspections, audits and compliance monitoring throughout delivery.

### Interface coordination

Multiple contractors and stakeholders were working across interconnected activities. Effective coordination and communication were essential to maintaining alignment and avoiding delays.

---

## Our approach

Lockrose deployed an integrated delivery team embedded within the client organisation, bringing together planning, construction assurance and quality management disciplines under a shared delivery objective.

### One team, shared outcome

Rather than operating as separate functions, each role contributed to a coordinated approach that provided a single view of programme performance, construction progress, quality compliance and project risk. The team comprised:

- Senior Planning Manager
- Construction Manager
- Three Tunnel Inspectors
- Quality Manager

### Strengthening programme controls

The Senior Planning Manager developed and maintained detailed delivery programmes, providing clear visibility of progress, critical dependencies and emerging risks. This improved confidence in programme forecasts, enabled proactive management of issues and supported informed decision-making throughout the project lifecycle.

### Providing construction assurance

The Construction Manager and Tunnel Inspectors maintained a continuous presence across critical works, providing technical oversight and monitoring construction activities as they progressed. Their involvement strengthened oversight of site operations, supported safe and efficient delivery and enabled issues to be identified and addressed before impacting the programme.

### Driving quality and compliance

The Quality Manager established robust quality assurance processes, including inspections, audits and compliance monitoring activities. Working closely with construction and planning teams, quality requirements were embedded throughout delivery, ensuring consistent standards and effective assurance across all activities.

### Creating a single view of delivery

By combining planning, construction assurance and quality management within one integrated team, Lockrose established a comprehensive view of project performance, risks and opportunities. This improved coordination across contractors and stakeholders, accelerated issue resolution and strengthened control of critical delivery activities.
